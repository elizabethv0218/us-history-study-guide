The Great Depression & WWII

Herbert Hoover vs. Franklin D. Roosevelt: Solutions to the Great Depression

  • Herbert Hoover (Republican 1928) believed that the producers and consumers of American economy was at fault and are responsible for fixing it. He believed that the public has to help themselves and the government should not be involved because he thought that the public would become too dependent on them and destroy their “spirit of liberty and progress”. The only thing the government should do in his eyes is to practice trickle down economics.
    • Trickle Down Economics is the process of the government putting money into the economy and surplus will trickle down towards the rest of society.
    • Hooverville were shanty towns built by homeless people during the Great Depression.
  • FDR (Democrat 1932) believed that big businesses increasing power dangers American society and the government should starts helping citizens directly.
  • He wanted the government to provide directly to the people and to stop free trade.

The New Deal

  • The New Deal was a series of domestic policies made by FDR in a measure to remove the U.S. from the Great Depression
    • FDR believed that broad executive power would allow him to do what is necessary to fix the U.S.
    • Actions made in the New Deal are categorized into three types of efforts
      • Relief: Giving direct aid to reduce suffering (the unemployed and the poor)
      • Relief: Giving direct aid to reduce suffering (the unemployed and the poor)
      • Reform: Change the financial system to ease the economic crisis
    • Major Actions Made In The New Deal
      • Bank Holiday (Recovery)
      • Emergency Banking Relief Act (Recovery)
      • Fireside Chats on radio (Relief)
      • Social Security Act (Relief)
      • Encourage unions (Reform)
      • Fair wages and working hours (Reform)
