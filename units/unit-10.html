Unit 10

The Great Depression & WWII

Herbert Hoover vs. Franklin D. Roosevelt: Solutions to the Great Depression

  • Herbert Hoover (Republican 1928) believed that the producers and consumers of American economy was at fault and are responsible for fixing it. He believed that the public has to help themselves and the government should not be involved because he thought that the public would become too dependent on them and destroy their “spirit of liberty and progress”. The only thing the government should do in his eyes is to practice trickle down economics.
    • Trickle Down Economics is the process of the government putting money into the economy and surplus will trickle down towards the rest of society.
    • Hooverville were shanty towns built by homeless people during the Great Depression.
  • FDR (Democrat 1932) believed that big businesses increasing power dangers American society and the government should starts helping citizens directly.
  • He wanted the government to provide directly to the people and to stop free trade.

The New Deal

  • The New Deal was a series of domestic policies made by FDR in a measure to remove the U.S. from the Great Depression
    • FDR believed that broad executive power would allow him to do what is necessary to fix the U.S.
    • Actions made in the New Deal are categorized into three types of efforts
      • Relief: Giving direct aid to reduce suffering (the unemployed and the poor)
      • Relief: Giving direct aid to reduce suffering (the unemployed and the poor)
      • Reform: Change the financial system to ease the economic crisis
    • Major Actions Made In The New Deal
      • Bank Holiday (Recovery)
      • Emergency Banking Relief Act (Recovery)
      • Fireside Chats on radio (Relief)
      • Social Security Act (Relief)
      • Encourage unions (Reform)
      • Fair wages and working hours (Reform)

FDR Broadened Executive Presidential Power

  • FDR added younger judges to the Supreme Court because he believed that younger people have personal experiences related to social and economic problems that would be solved by the New Deal, and would agree with him as a result.

African Americans and the New Deal

  • The Works Progress Administration offered work relief programs and achieved new visibility in the government.
  • The Agricultural Adjustment Administration acreage reduction made life worse.
  • Segregated camps continued to exist.
  • FDR did nothing to stop lynching and the poll tax.

Women and the New Deal

  • The Works Progress Administration allowed women to work by making hot lunches for school children.
  • Eleanor Roosevelt spoke out for women being active in the workforce.

U.S. Involvement in WWII

  • U.S. neutrality was abandoned because they started to help Great Britain by lending them materials and placed an embargo on oil on Japan.
  • Pearl Harbor was bombed by the Japanese, causing the destruction of 21 U.S. ships and the deaths of 2,000 people.

U.S. Homefront During WWII

  • Factories produced 60% of Allied ammunition.
  • Resources were rationed War bonds were created.
  • Homegrown foods were encouraged.
  • Women became 25% of the nation's labor force.
  • African and Native Americans were forced into helping fight for the U.S..
  • War Power Act allowed the censoring of mail and the reorganization of federal agencies.
  • Because most people had jobs, the U.S. was no longer in the Great Depression.

Japanese Internment Camps

  • U.S. Justifications
    • Uncertainty and fear that situations like Pearl Harbor may reappear
    • Believed that it was a military necessity
    • Believed that it was necessary for national security
  • Executive Order 9066 allowed the military to have power to limit civil liberties of Japanese Americans.

Debate of Usage of the Atomic Bomb in War

  • The Manhattan Project led to the development of the atomic bomb.
  • President Harry Truman looked to his advisors for help, but they had conflicting ideas.
  • Truman in the end decided to drop the bomb twice, one in Hiroshima, and one in Nagasaki.
  • After the atomic bombs were used, WWII ended.
