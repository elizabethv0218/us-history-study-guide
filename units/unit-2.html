Unit 2

A New Nation

Articles of Confederation (1781)

  • The first form of national government created by the Founding Fathers (after the Revolutionary War) who believed that British rule held too much authority over the lives of Americans.

  • The Founding Fathers wanted to form a government that placed power in the hands of the people and made way for the development of strong state governments.

Running the Country

  • There was no national army, but there was a state militia instead.

  • There was no federal taxation, even though they were in debt. The states were depended on for contributions.

  • There was no executive leader. Only the legislative branch, Congress, existed.

  • No one executed the laws (no dictator / monarch, no checks and balances).

  • Each state only had one vote. State population was not considered. States could pass laws, but not everyone's voice would be heard.

  • Unanimous vote was needed to:
    • amend the Articles of Confederation
    • attempt to have the states work & agree with each other

  • Amends would be a long process if all the states did not agreed.

  • The Declaration of Independence was a statement and list of grievances by colonists to officially break from Great Britain.

Shay's Rebellion

Shay's Rebellion left the U.S. in debt because farmers suffered from low crop prices and growing debts & taxes. Creditors loaned money and wanted debtors (farmers) to pay them back.

Paper currency was believed to be helpful for the farmers. When Massachusetts legislature ignored farmers demands for paper currency, more time to pay back debts, and abolish jail-time for debts, a revolt led by Daniel Shays occurred. Farmers joined Shays because they were afraid they'd lose their farms.

African Americans in the American Revolution

African Americans saw freedom in England, unlike the tyranny in the U.S. This motivated them to enlist in support of the revolutionary cause. African Americans travelled North or to mixed race settlements because they wanted liberty too.

At the end, many received their freedom, but not everyone. The North even petitioned for their freedom. In the South there were violent uprisings. There were more African American soldiers on the English side compared to the American side. The U.S. did not want them to be armed and many viewed them as being exploited by the British.

Women in the Revolutionary War

During President John Adams’ presidency, his wife Abigail Adams wanted him to remember the ladies because, at the time, women were seen as domestic workers. Her letter to Adams says that all men can be tyrants if they have power and that women are capable of rebelling if they are not fairly represented or heard.

The Virginia Plan vs. The New Jersey Plan

  • Shay's Rebellion highlighted the Article's weaknesses. James Madison, Alexander Hamilton and George Washington wanted a stronger government.

  • The Virginia Plan: proposed a bicameral legislative branch.

  • The New Jersey Plan: congressional representation (one vote per one state).

  • Constitutional Convention (1787) - 60 politicians met to draft a new constitution to strengthen the federal government, national security, and order of the citizens. The greatest struggle was to figure out a way to handle representation.

The Great Compromise

The Great Compromise called for 2 Houses, bicameralism, the Senate and the House of Representatives. Delegates approved this on July 16, 1787.

  • The Senate gives equal representation to all the states which satisfied small states.

  • The House of Representatives was based on state population to satisfy large states.

  • Debate arose over whether slaves should be included in the population which was sadly the least divisive subject at the time.

  • The Compromise was a combination of the New Jersey and Virginia Plans.

The Three Fifths Compromise

  • States with slaves wanted to count their slaves for more representation. Southern states did not want them to be counted because their taxes would increase. The northern delegates disagreed. Many people believed blacks and whites should be equal in representation because their labor is valuable. The South viewed slaves as property for taxes. Opponents of slavery said that slaves don’t vote, therefore, they shouldn't be represented.

    In the end, 3 out of 5 slaves would be added to the population for representation in the House of Representatives.

  • The law declared that there would be no importation of slaves starting from the year 1808 and onwards.

Powers of Government

  • Federalism power is divided between a national government and the state governments.

  • Division of Power delegates created a new government that was a form of federalism.
    • Powers granted to the national government by the Constitution are known as delegated powers, or enumerated powers. They can control foreign affairs, like regulate trade between states.
    • Other powers are kept by the states and called reserved powers. They provide for and supervise education.
    • Both share the right to tax and establish courts.

  • Federalists, or nationalists, supported the new constitution. They said their opponents did not offer another constitution and only gave critiques. Delegates of the constitutional convention were federalists. They believed they were more unified, organized, and better able to manage the national debate. They favored a strong federal government.

  • Opponents were Anti-federalists. They believed giving the government more power would make them grow corrupt and tyrannical.

The Bill of Rights

The public knew there was an absence of a bill of rights to protect the people and the states from the national government.

  • James Madison proposed constitutional amendments to protect individual rights.
  • Thomas Jefferson believed they were necessary and should not be violated.
  • Congress approved 12 amendments. 3/4 of the states approved 10 of the 12 amendments known as the bill of rights. The Bill of Rights is the first 10 Amendments to the Constitution (civil liberties)

  1. You have the right to establish and practice any religion, speech, press, & peacefully protest.

  2. There will be a militia for security of the State. People can keep and bear arms.

  3. Soldiers will not be quartered without the consent of the property owner.

  4. There will be no unreasonable searches or seizures of a person and their possessions. Authorities are required to have a warrant with probable cause from a court indicating who and what will be searched/ seized.

  5. No one will be charged for the same crime twice or forced to be a witness against himself. You cannot be denied life or property.

  6. You have the right to a trial by an impartial jury where the crime was committed. You should be informed of the accusation, confront the witnesses against you, allowed to obtain witnesses in your favor and have the right to a lawyer.

  7. You have the right to a trial by jury.

  8. There will be no excessive bail, fines, or cruel/ unusual punishments imposed

  9. The people are guaranteed other rights that are not explicitly stated in the Bill of Rights.

  10. The federal government has powers granted by the constitution. Everything else is left to the states or the people.

The Branches of Government

  1. The Legislative Branch
    The Legislative Branch makes laws. The House of Representatives and Senate can veto each other's bills. They approve presidential nominations (i.e. Justices) and control money. The branch can pass laws overriding the president's veto and impeach the president.

  2. The Executive Branch
    The Executive Branch enforces laws. It includes the President, the Vice President, and the President’s Cabinet. The President can veto congressional legislation and nominate Supreme Court judges.

  3. The Judicial Branch
    The Judicial Branch interprets the law in Judicial Review. The branch includes the Supreme court, the Court of Appeals, and District Courts. They can declare laws and presidential actions unconstitutional.
  • The purpose of Checks and Balances is to ensure that the 3 branches of government share equal power.

Constitutional Flexibility

Constitutional Flexibility says that the Constitution is capable of meeting the changing needs of Americans. Article 1 Section 8 gives Congress the power to make laws that are necessary and proper. This is known as the elastic clause because it stretches the government’s power. It allows the authority of the government to expand and meet unforeseen circumstances. The Constitution can be formally changed through amendments.

The amendment process allowed the Constitution to deal with major changes to the government. Only 27 Amendments have been added and they’ve helped deal with national crisis while preserving rights. People have very little say in the Amendments because the Founding Fathers feared direct democracy.

Cases of Judicial Review

  • Obergefell v. Hodges
    Ohio didn’t recognize the gay marriage of Obergefell and John Arthur who married in Maryland. Obergefell was not listed as a Arthur's surviving spouse. South Carolina ruled in favor of marriage equality and overturned the ban on same sex marriage. These bans were unconstitutional because they violated due process and the equal protection clause.

  • McCulloch v. Maryland
    The U.S. government had a national bank in 1791. Alexander Hamilton believed the government had the power to make another bank. Thomas Jefferson disagreed. President James Madison believed the country needed a national bank and asked Congress to make a second one which was approved. The states opposed these national banks because they competed with state banks, bank managers were believed to be corrupt, and they believed the government was becoming more powerful. Maryland passed a law to force outside banks to pay a state tax of $15,000 to close down the Baltimore branch. James McCulloch worked in that branch and refused to pay. Maryland believed it had the power to tax any business in its state. Their rationale was that if the national government could regulate state banks, then Maryland is allowed to regulate national banks. The Constitution doesn't let Congress create a national bank. McCulloch was convicted so he appealed to the Maryland Court of Appeals. His lawyer said the national bank was necessary and proper. Therefore, Maryland cannot tax the national bank because it hinders the national bank’s job. The court agreed in favor of McCulloch. In the end, Federal laws beat state laws.

  • Gibbons v. Ogden
    Robert Fulton and Robert Livingston had a monopoly for steamboats on the waterways of New York. The monopoly was given by the New York state government because it was very profitable. Aaron Ogden had a Fulton-Livingston license and operated between New Jersey and New York. Thomas Gibson didn’t have this license, but he had a federal coasting license. The waterway was interstate, so commerce trade was across states. Ogden believed New York should control the waterway. Gibbons argued that Congress had power over the waterway. If each state made laws about interstate commerce there would be chaos. Ogden won in New York. The Supreme Court ruled in favor of Gibbons because Congress had the power to regulate these operations. Once again, the federal license overpowers the state license.

  • Texas v. Johnson
    Gregory Lee Johnson burned an American flag to protest against President Ronald Reagan. The laws in Texas said that that was a criminal offense, so he was convicted. This is a violation of his first Amendment.

  • Mall v. Ohio
    Cleveland officers received a tip that Miss Map was in possession of many gambling slips, and that a bomber was hiding in her home. Police arrived with no warrant and left, but they returned and went inside without a warrant. Bomb making materials were found and she was arrested. This was a violation of her 4th Amendment.

  • New Jersey v. T.L.O.
    A teacher saw two girls smoking in the bathroom, so they were taken to the principal's office. The vice principal searched one of the student’s purse and found cigarettes, rolling paper, marijuana, a pipe, plastic bags, money, lists of names, letters which were signs of drug dealing. The student said that was unconstitutional violated her 4th Amendment. However, it was a reasonable search and the safety of the students had to be ensured.

  • Unwritten Constitution Ideas and Procedures
    • Cabinet of advisors were created because they helped President Washington make decisions and carry policies. Today the Cabinet consists of Secretaries.
    • Political parties. They originate from the debate between Alexander Hamilton, who led the federalists, and Thomas Jefferson, who led the Democratic-Republican Party.
