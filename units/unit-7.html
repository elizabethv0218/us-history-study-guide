Unit 7

Reconstruction

The Progressive Era was a time of widespread social activism and political/economic reform and addressed issues from Industrialization

The Progressive movement aimed to restore economic opportunities and correct injustices in American life

  • Progressive Impulse
    • Anti monopole = fear of concentrated power and the urge to limit and disperse authority and wealth
    • Belief in the importance of social cohesion: that people are shaped by their environment and the welfare of any person depending on the welfare of the whole society
  • 4 Pillars of the Progressive Era
    • Promote social welfare (help the needy)
    • Promote Moral Improvement (standards of society)
    • Create Economic Reform (reevaluate capitalism)
    • Fostering efficiency
  • Rise of Labor Unions
    • Labor Union efforts using Collective Bargaining
      • Fight against harsh working conditions
      • Increase amount of breaks/vacations
      • Increase amount of wages
  • Muckrakers are journalists/activists who exposed injustices and corruption in politics, business and/or society
    • Roosevelt personally supported muckrakers
    • They directed public attention to social, economic, and political injustices
      • Ex: political corruption, stock market manipulation, fake advertising, vices (immoral behavior), impure food and drugs, racial discrimination, lynching
    • Examples of Muckrakers
      • Ida Tarbell was a writer for the McClause magazine that addressed the injustice in the oil company and that John D. Rockefeller monopolized the industry (Addressed creating economic reform)
      • Ida B. Wells was a journalist and teacher of segregated schools that led the first anti-lynching campaign (Addressed promoting moral improvement)
      • Jacob Riis addressed the poor quality of tenements, the hardships of the poor, and child labor
      • Jane Addams addressed the everyday needs of poor and the newly arrived european immigrants
      • Lincoln Steffens addressed corruption in political systems, especially in cities (political machines)
      • Upton Sinclair wrote The Jungle to try to improve working conditions. This book resulted in the Meat Inspection Act and the Pure Food and Drug Act
    • The Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire was a fire that began in a factory filled with 600 women workers that was at the top three floors of the Asch Building
      • Deaths:
        • 49 = burned or suffocated
        • 36 = died in the elevator shaft
        • 58 = died jumping out of the windows
        • Total = 145 people
      • The owners of the factory wnt on trial and was found not guilty
      • Reforms made
        • Municipal Building Code → required safety devices
        • Workers Union → marched to protest
