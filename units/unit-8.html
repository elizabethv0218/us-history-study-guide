Unit 8

U.S. Imperialism

American Imperialism/Expansionism

  • Imperialism is the foreign policy that allows one one country’s control over another weaker one
  • Annexation is the process of taking land with consent
  • Cessesion is the process of taking land after winning a war
  • Motivations for an imperialistic foreign policy
    • Promoting Economic Growth
    • Protecting American Security
    • Preserving American Spirit
    • Social Darwinism

The Monroe Doctrine

  • Main Points
    • The US cannot be colonized by any European country
    • Europe cannot have control over any part of the Western Hemisphere
    • US agrees to stay out of European politics
    • Existing European colonies in the Western Hemisphere can stay untouched by the US
  • By using the Monroe Doctrine, US is able to reserve all land in the Western Hemisphere all to themselves. This allows them to have the ability to grow their power at whatever pace they like.

Spanish-American War

  • Possible causes
    • Spain is interfering in the Western Hemisphere by having a war in Cuba for Cuban Independence. As it states in the Monroe Doctrine, the US can intervene if they feel threatened.
    • Cuba was struggling for freedom
    • Spain created the “Reconcentration Policy” to send Cubans to concentration camps
    • The USS Maine, a US Navy ship, was blown up, and Spain was blamed for it
  • Outcomes
    • The US gained the Philippines, Guam, Puerto Rico, Wake Island, Johnston Island, and Palmyra Island

Yellow Journalism

  • Yellow Journalism is journalism based on sensationalism and exaggeration
    • Examples of Yellow Journalists:
      • William Randolph Hearst was a journalist for his New York Journal
      • Joseph Pulitzer was a journalist for his New York World
      • Hearst and Pulitzer blamed the Spanish for the sinking of the USS Maine without any evidence because they wanted to sell more copies and produce a larger profit from their newspapers.
      • There were many different interpretations of the explosion of the USS Maine. Some just stated the facts of the event and some made exaggerated conclusions of who was at fault.

Philippines and America

  • Annexations of the Philippines
    • As the Philippines was fighting for independence from Spain, Emilio Aguinaldo, leader of the Filipino forces, cooperated with the US and supported them during the Spanish-American War so he can declare independence on the Philippines.
    • The Treaty of Paris brought a formal end to the Spanish-American War and ceded territorial ownership of the Philippines to the US.
  • The Philippine-American War
    • The US was debating whether or not to annex the Philippines, so Emilio Aguinaldo declared war on the US.
    • The US won and the Philippines was under their control from 1902 to 1946.

Annexation of Hawaii

  • Lili’uokalani, a former queen of Hawaii, was overthrown by the Committee on Annexation because they thought that annexation by the US would benefit Hawaii’s economy

The Roosevelt Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine (Big Stick Policy)

  • The Roosevelt Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine (Big Stick Policy) was a proposition made by Roosevelt that was added to the Monroe Doctrine
    • Main Points
      • The U.S. just wants other countries to be stable and to be doing well.
      • The U.S. will only intervene if they see that a country is not capable of governing themselves and make bad decisions.
      • The U.S. will act as the police of the Western Hemisphere and keep it in law to their rules and rights.
      • The U.S. should have a strong navy just in case if they are threatened.
      • The U.S. will use force on Latin America to protect their economic profit.
        • They will use military force to make sure debts are paid.
    • Intervention in Panama
      • Roosevelt sent an American warship to help Panama win independence against Columbia.
      • Once Panama became independent, Roosevelt declared Panama under US protection and ownership of land needed to build the Panama Canal.

Dollar Diplomacy

  • Dollar Diplomacy was a policy aimed at furthering the interests of the US abroad.
    • It encouraged the investment of the US capital in foreign countries.
    • It guaranteed loans made to Central and South America by American business people in exchange for control of their processes like taxes and banks.

Open Door Policy

  • The Open Door Policy favored equal trading rights for all foreign nations in China.
    • The Boxer Rebellion was an uprising in Northern China against the spread of Western and Japanese influence
